## Definition

Engagement is the process of purposefully interacting with, attending to, developing a relationship with and interest in an object of study, community, project, and/or assortment of tasks.

## Role within the GLQF

Engagement is one of three overarching constructs — alongside Knowledge and Integration — that together distinguish college- and university-level learning. Where Knowledge captures *what* a learner has acquired, Engagement captures the *depth and quality of the learner's relationship* with that material, with collaborators, and with the broader community.

The GLQF treats Engagement as a signal of undergraduate-level learning because knowledge, skills, and abilities can be acquired at any level. Purposeful, sustained engagement — with ideas, with people, with civic and ethical questions — is part of what makes learning recognisable as college work.

## How Engagement shows up across the eight domains

Engagement is expected to bridge all eight learning domains. It is most directly visible in:

- **Communication** — exchanging ideas with audiences and collaborators
- **Sociocultural and Civic Engagement** — interacting with diverse communities and perspectives
- **Learning Engagement** — taking ownership of one's own learning process

But evaluators look for engagement everywhere — in how a learner pursues specialized knowledge, takes on ethical questions, navigates information sources, or applies what they know.

## What to look for as evidence

- Sustained, purposeful interaction with a topic over time, not just one-off exposure
- Discussion, dialogue, or collaboration with others around the material
- Service learning, volunteering, community work, or leadership tied to the area of study
- Goal-setting, reflection, and use of feedback to deepen the work
- Evidence of genuine interest — questions pursued, new directions tried, curiosity acted on
