## Definition

Knowledge is the culmination of facts, information, concepts, principles, skills and competencies that a person acquires through experiences and education that result in the theoretical and practical understanding of an area.

## Role within the GLQF

Knowledge is one of three overarching constructs — alongside Integration and Engagement — that together describe college- and university-level learning. It encompasses both *theoretical* understanding (concepts, principles, frameworks) and *practical* understanding (skills, competencies, applied techniques) within a given area.

Knowledge alone, however, is not sufficient to indicate college-level learning. The GLQF was built precisely because knowledge, skills, and abilities can exist at any educational level. The framework expects Knowledge to appear in concert with Engagement and Integration in order to qualify a body of learning as undergraduate.

## How Knowledge shows up across the eight domains

Knowledge is expressed most directly through three learning domains:

- **Specialized Knowledge** — depth within a particular discipline or profession
- **Applied Knowledge** — putting that knowledge to work in real situations
- **Integrated Knowledge** — connecting it across contexts and disciplines

But Knowledge underlies all eight domains. Communication, Information Literacy, Ethical Responsibility, Sociocultural and Civic Engagement, and Learning Engagement all require a base of knowledge in order to be demonstrated meaningfully.

## What to look for as evidence

- Accurate use of the field's concepts, terminology, and methods
- Ability to explain *why* a practice or principle works, not just *how* to follow it
- Awareness of how the field has evolved and where its current boundaries sit
- Work samples, certificates, publications, presentations, or analyses that document understanding
- The ability to teach the material to someone else
